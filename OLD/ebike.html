Case study

Revel’s Electric Bike Subscription Program

Revel rolled out a pilot electric bike subscription program and for my part I designed the onboarding, purchase funnel, and dashboard for Revel’s mobile app.

Overview

By 2020 Revel had firmly established itself as NYC's premeire solution to last-mile mobility through it's network of shared mopeds - but the company had larger aspirations. Cavnue was looking into ways to branch farther out into the world of electric mobility.

It was decided that we would embark on a pilot program to test the efficacy of an electric bike rental program. The idea was we would allow people in the NYC region to sign up and pay for a subscription plan that would give them access to a bike that we would deliver and maintain.

For my part I worked closely with the biz dev team and other product stakeholders to shape the scope of the project and how the user experience should play out. I also lead the design of the sign up, order funnel, and subscription dashboard.

Summary
Problem

Revel, intending to diversify its product offerings, has ambition to branch out into other forms of electric micro-mobility.

Solution

Launch an innovative ebike subscription program that allows users to order and keep a premium electric bike for the duration of their subscription.

My Role

Collaborated with team to formulate the scope of the program and also owned the design of the user flow.

Initial research

We collaborated with IDEO on a week-long design sprint that helped us identify who were our core user personas as well as what constituted their specific needs and how we could efficiently service them.

The process involved countless interviews with stakeholders in various industries surrounding vehicle travel and road maintenance.

Documentation

Once we had a clear idea of whom we were building for and what they needed, I, along with the rest of the team, began a process of meticulously documenting our findings in order to share them with the rest of the company.

We took it upon ourselves as the product team to steer the direction of the company’s efforts and keep the end user front of mind.

Wireframing

I iterated through countless wireframes in order to narrow down the scope of the project. As I did this I continuously checked in with engineering stakeholders to make sure all features stayed in scope.

A major forcing function of our design process was the fact that the hardware specs were being worked through concurrently with the front-end designs.

MVP

Eventually we landed on an MVP design that accounted for a strong foundational solution that accounted for a core set of features that occupied a sweet spot of user needs and technical limitations.

MVP

The solution included live and archival views of incoming data streams, a feed of AI generated ‘road events’ and a live monitoring tool that telegraphs the status of every deployed piece of hardware.

Overview

Upon release, the Cavanue Dashboard was a crucial step in proving the value prop of the company. It allowed outside stakeholders to understand the capabilities of a deployed system of sensors, it gave ML engineers a means to acquire crucial data without resorting to 3rd party tools, and it allowed anyone with access to glean at a high level the overall health of the technology.

In many ways it served as a milestone for the company - proving the efficacy of Cavnue's mission.
