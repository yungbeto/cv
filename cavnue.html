Case study

Cavnue Live Dashboard

A web-based dashboard that provides insight into a mesh network of autonomous vehicle sensors

Overview

Cavnue is on a mission to bridge the gap between current autonomous vehicle capabilities and the promised future of full self-driving.

When I was brought on to the product team, our main goal was to take the overwhelming amount of valuable data generated by Cavnue's hardware system and surface it in a way that was valuable to our end user.

For my part I lead the design of a dashboard that not only provided valuable insight to a user, but also telegraphed the accuracy of a given data point, thereby instilling a level of confidence that Cavnue's tech is not only powerful but reliable.

My work resulted in moving away from third party tools, saving the company valuable capital, and laying the groundwork for ensuring the tools we built foregrounded data transparency and fostered a high level of trust from our users.

Summary
Problem

The Cavnue system of networked hardware that monitors traffic throughput in a given section of road generates a lot of data. How might we surface this data in a way that benefits our end-users?

Solution

Develop a sophisticated dashboard that provides valuable insights into the generated data in a way that’s relavent to a given user persona.

My Role

Worked closely with stakeholders to research and develop not only specific user personas and their respective requirements, but design a set of features that targets specific needs.

Initial research

We collaborated with IDEO on a week-long design sprint that helped us identify who were our core user personas as well as what constituted their specific needs and how we could efficiently service them.

The process involved countless interviews with stakeholders in various industries surrounding vehicle travel and road maintenance.

Documentation

Once we had a clear idea of whom we were building for and what might constitute their needs I along with the rest of the team began a process of meticulously documenting our findings in order to share them with the rest of the company.

We took it upon ourselves as the product team to steer the direction of the company’s efforts and keep the end user front of mind.

Wireframing

As with any design project I iterated through countless wireframes in order to narrow down the scope of the project. As I did this I continuously checked in with engineering stakeholders to make sure all features stayed within the realm of possibility.

A major forcing function of our design process was the fact that the hardware specs were being worked through concurrently with the front-end designs meaning each work stream was influencing the other.

MVP

Eventually we landed on an MVP design that accounted for a strong foundational solution that accounted for a core set of features that occupied a sweet spot of user needs and technical limitations.

In the hot seat

The solution included live and archival views of incoming data streams, a feed of AI generated ‘road events’ and a live monitoring tool that telegraphs the status of every deployed piece of hardware.

Results

The passenger tablet provided an engaging value proposition and memorable product distinction. The tablet could have benefited from taking more time to QA before our initial release, however, that was a compromise we consciously made to meet an aggressive deadline.


Ultimately, due to regulatory circumstances out of our control, we were forced to sunset the tablet. However, during its brief tenure, the tablet generated high engagement numbers and, I believe, could have proven to be a very valuable customer touchpoint had it had more time to mature.
